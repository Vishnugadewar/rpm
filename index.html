Target Users

Project Scope

  • Mobile application for electricity usage management
  • Integration with smart home devices
  • AI-powered recommendations and automation
  • 12-week development timeframe
  • Initial focus on residential users

The Energy Management Problem

Visibility Challenges

Most users have no visibility into which devices consume the most energy and typically only become aware of consumption when the monthly bill arrives.

Control Limitations

Difficulty managing energy usage in multi-resident households where manual tracking efforts are perceived as ineffective.

Awareness Gaps

Uncertainty about optimal timing for appliance usage and no clear connection between behavior and energy consumption.

Key Research Questions

  • What are the primary pain points users face when managing home energy consumption?
  • What features would make tracking and reducing energy consumption easier?
  • How do users want AI to assist them in managing electricity usage?
  • How can the application effectively serve households with multiple occupants?

Competitive Analysis

Based on the competitive analysis on 7 companies (Google Nest, Amazon Smart Plugs, Ecobee, Emporia Energy, Sense, Eve Systems, and Aliste Technologies), all companies offer energy monitoring and device-level monitoring capabilities, as well as smart home integration.

However we did find none of the companies currently offers weather-adaptive energy optimization. We belive it is a potential opportunity for our system.

Key Research Findings

Desired Features

  • Real-time device-specific energy tracking
  • Hourly usage and cost tracking
  • Simple, visual dashboards
  • AI-powered usage pattern analysis
  • Automated scheduling based on routines
  • Anomaly detection and alerts

User Preferences

  • Push notifications for excessive consumption
  • Remote device control capabilities
  • Personalized energy-saving tips
  • Clear visual representations of data
  • Proactive but not intrusive AI
  • Manual control options

Market Gaps

  • Most solutions lack personalized AI recommendations
  • Few address multi-user households effectively
  • Limited real-time device-specific data
  • Complex interfaces for non-technical users
  • Poor integration between tracking and actions

Major UX Insights

  • Transparency is Key: Users value visibility into consumption more than automated control.
  • Shared Living Challenges: Multi-resident households face unique energy management issues.
  • Notification Preferences: Users prefer timely, relevant, actionable alerts.
  • Visualization Matters: Simple, clear visual representations are strongly preferred.
  • AI Expectations: Users want proactive but not intrusive AI with manual control.

10. Reflection

Challenges Faced

Throughout our UX Research and Requirements Development process, we encountered several challenges. Initially, creating effective survey and interview questions that would gather meaningful functional requirements was difficult for us. We made a lot of mistakes and revised our research instruments multiple times during the early phases of our research. However with persistent research and valuable feedback from Dr. Nwokeji, we eventually overcame this obstacle and developed appropriate tools for data collection. Another challenge was securing a diverse demographic of participants. Our interviewee pool was predominantly composed of individuals with technical backgrounds, which created difficulties in gathering functional requirements suitable for non-tech-savvy users.

Lessons Learned

The research process we believe significantly deepened our understanding of user-centered design principles. During our initial brainstorming sessions, we frequently doubted and second-guessed our feature selections and design choices. However, as we progressed through multiple rounds of surveys, interviews, and testingâ€”accumulating more user data with each iterationâ€”the elements for our system began to align collectively. This demonstrated the power of user-driven design decisions.

Additionally, we developed valuable technical skills throughout this project, particularly mastering tools like Figma for developing high-fidelity prototypes. This is an important professional skill that will serve us well in future UI/UX development.
